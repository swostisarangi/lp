PAIN-VAIN

The day started out pretty sunny but gradually became cloudy and dark.
The day was usual for Vivek. Wake up early, have his breakfast, finish
his assignments, if he has any, have a chat with his friend Manu over
phone and finally settle down doing nothing. The lockdown came down
quite heavily on Vivek and his friends as they had nothing much to do
and they couldn’t meet each other either. COVID-19 was certainly a
bane for them and more wretched were the social distancing norms that
prevented them from meeting up.

But Vivek was a tad more fortunate as his father ran a small restaurant
in the city and he used to take Vivek with him. However, there was a caveat
to this seemingly fabulous plan. Vivek was just allowed to step out of
his house and not out of the small restaurant. Alas! No soul can actually
condole with him. He has to sit at the counter with a mask on and that
too for many hours while his father was away completing the deliveries.
It was even more boring and painstaking for him to do this. But he didn’t
have a choice as his mom was sick and couldn’t do the job.
